When to Keep Captive, When to Transition to India, When to Outsource 
Conglomerate's view, grounded in 40+ GBS-inclusive GCC engagements: 
▪ Keep captive (in the U.S.): Roles requiring deep institutional knowledge of BOSE-specific processes 
not yet documented, or senior stakeholder relationship management that cannot be effectively 
transitioned in the near term. 
▪ Transition to India GCC: Standardised, high -volume GBS processes : Order Management, Customer 
Master Data, Operational Procurement, Product Master Data. India provides a huge cost advantage 
for these roles, with equivalent or better quality when supported by a structured knowledge transfer. 
▪ Outsource: Consider only for non -core, low -differentiation processes where volume does not justify 
captive investment. Conglomerate's position: outsourcing GBS core should be a last resort, not a 
default, it surrenders process ownership, data, and institutional knowledge. 
The Five Critical Decisions BOSE Must Make Before Launch 
▪ Purpose – the vision, broader goals and the expected outcomes – KPIs & Governance Model  
▪ Ownership Model - must decide how much control you want from Day 1 versus the speed of entry.  
▪ Location Strategy - selecting the right city balances talent availability, cost, and employee retention.  
▪ Operating Model - which & what combination of GCC, BOT, and partner-supported engagement model 
with specifics & the vendor consolidation (if any)  
 
▪ Effective Risk Management – for the parent organisation, must have a plan of action to transition from 
"Risk Exposure" (during the initial setup phase) to "Risk Resilience" (in the path towards maturity).  
 
Taken together, these five decisions will do more than shape the initial launch model; they will define the 
quality, resilience, and long -term value of BOSE’s India GCC. In our experience, organizations that align 
early on purpose, ownership, location, operating model, and risk strategy can move with greater confidence, 
reduce avoidable execution friction, and build a centre that delivers both near-term efficiency and sustained 
strategic impact.  
 
Put simply, getting these decisions right at the outset materially increases the likelihood that BOSE’s GCC 
evolves as intended—from a delivery engine into a true capability and innovation platform.

Establishing a high -scale Global Capability Centre (GCC) that unifies  Information Technology (IT), 
Business Global Services (BGS), and Research & Development (R&D)  is one of the most powerful 
strategic moves an international enterprise can make to drive operational efficiency, accelerate execution, 
and sustain long-term innovation. 
 
However, the traditional standalone (DIY) route often involves significant upfront capital commitments and 
regulatory lead times of 12 to 18 months, while conventional outsourcing can create unacceptable 
intellectual property (IP) and control risks.  ConglomerateIT’s cost-plus Build-Operate-Transfer (BOT) 
framework offers an enterprise-grade alternative that enables immediate launch, reduces expansion 
risk, and preserves strategic control. 
 
The Recommendation: ConglomerateIT's Unified Cost-Plus BOT Model 
 
This model provides BOSE with a unified operating structure through which IT, BGS, and R&D functions 
can scale concurrently while retaining governance discipline, cost transparency, and decision rights. Under 
a cost-plus commercial construct, BOSE bears the actual costs with a fixed management fee, creating line-
of-sight into the underlying cost base and avoiding the opaque margin structures often embedded in 
traditional outsourcing arrangements. ConglomerateIT assumes responsibility for the local operating 
layer—including infrastructure, compliance, and workforce, etc.thereby allowing BOSE to focus on strategic 
oversight, functional performance, and long-term capability development. 
 
This GCC-as-a-Service model is expected to deliver 40–60% cost savings relative to equivalent home-
country operations, while reducing operating costs by ~15–25% compared with a self-managed captive 
centre or traditional large-vendor outsourcing model in India. 
 
Accordingly, the proposed model may be characterised as a Managed GCC delivered through a Hosted 
BOT framework. Within this construct, ConglomerateIT would charge a management fee of ~ 15% over 
the actual cost base, providing a transparent commercial structure that supports local execution, 
compliance, and operating governance. 
 
4.5 India Location Strategy 
 
In the architecture of a Global Capability Centre (GCC), location selection is no longer a mere real estate 
or cost-saving decision; it is the single most critical determinant of long-term operational resilience, 
cultural alignment, and scaling velocity. For a U.S. enterprise looking to expand its footprint, a poorly 
optimized location strategy introduces immediate bottlenecks—such as hyper-competition for talent, 
infrastructure constraints, and rising operating overhead—that can degrade product roadmaps and erode 
the financial advantages of offshoring. 
An effective location strategy balances four vital operational pillars to ensure the GCC functions as a 
high-performing extension of the parent organization. 
ConglomerateIT Location Intelligence, How We Know What We Know 
ConglomerateIT’s location assessment is designed to help BOSE make a city choice that supports both 
immediate launch success and long-term GCC maturity. Our in-house experts maintain quarterly-updated 
intelligence across all four shortlisted cities, covering talent supply, salary movement, attrition trends, 
business ecosystem signals, and operating conditions. Importantly, this view is not derived from static
